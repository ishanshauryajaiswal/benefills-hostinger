## Core Thyroid & Hormone Concepts

### Thyroid Basics

**What the thyroid does:**
- Produces T3 and T4 hormones that regulate metabolism, energy, body temperature, heart rate, and more
- Controlled by TSH (thyroid-stimulating hormone) from the pituitary gland

**Common thyroid disorders:**
1. **Hypothyroidism** (underactive thyroid)
   - Symptoms: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, brain fog, hair loss, constipation
   - Most common cause: Hashimoto's thyroiditis (autoimmune)
   - Treatment: Levothyroxine (synthetic T4) or other thyroid medications

2. **Hashimoto's Thyroiditis**
   - Autoimmune condition attacking the thyroid
   - Can cause hypothyroidism over time
   - Often managed with medication + lifestyle (diet, stress, sleep)

3. **Hyperthyroidism** (overactive thyroid)
   - Symptoms: Anxiety, weight loss, rapid heartbeat, heat intolerance
   - Common cause: Graves' disease
   - Note: Benefills focuses primarily on *hypo*thyroid and hormone support

### Related Hormonal Conditions

**PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome):**
- Hormonal disorder affecting ovaries, insulin, and androgens
- Symptoms: Irregular periods, insulin resistance, weight gain, acne, hair growth
- Nutrition support: Blood sugar stability, anti-inflammatory foods, nutrient density

**Postpartum Thyroiditis:**
- Temporary thyroid inflammation after pregnancy (can cause hyper- then hypothyroidism)
- Often resolves but may become permanent hypothyroidism
- Key needs: Gentle support, safe nutrition, monitoring

**Adrenal/HPA Axis Dysfunction:**
- Chronic stress affects cortisol and can worsen thyroid symptoms
- Adaptogens (ashwagandha, rhodiola) may help modulate stress response

**Protein:**
- Supports metabolism, satiety, and muscle maintenance (important when metabolism is slow)
- Aim for adequate intake at each meal

- Critical for hormone production and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins
- Focus on omega-3s (anti-inflammatory) and monounsaturated fats

- Supports gut health, blood sugar stability, and regular digestion
- Important for PCOS and hypothyroid (often struggle with constipation)

## Evidence-Based Dietary Patterns

### What Research Supports

**Selenium supplementation:**
- Studies show selenium (200mcg/day) may reduce thyroid antibodies in Hashimoto's patients
- Food sources preferred over high-dose supplements (Brazil nuts, seafood, poultry)

**Anti-inflammatory diet:**
- Mediterranean-style eating linked to better thyroid and metabolic health
- Emphasizes whole foods, healthy fats, lean protein, colorful vegetables

**Blood sugar stability:**
- Especially important for PCOS and insulin resistance
- Balanced meals (protein + fat + fiber) prevent energy crashes

**Adaptogens:**
- Ashwagandha shown in some studies to support stress response and may help with subclinical hypothyroidism
- Generally safe but should consult doctor if on thyroid meds

### What to Approach Cautiously

**Goitrogens (cruciferous vegetables, soy):**
- Raw cruciferous (broccoli, kale, etc.) *in large amounts* may interfere with iodine uptake
- **Reality:** Cooking reduces goitrogen effect; most people can eat these foods safely
- **Message:** "Don't fear broccoli‚Äîjust eat a varied diet"

**Gluten:**
- Some Hashimoto's patients report feeling better gluten-free (possible cross-reactivity)
- **Not universally necessary**; recommend testing and working with doctor

**Iodine supplementation:**
- Too much iodine can worsen Hashimoto's
- Most people get enough from diet; supplementation should be medically supervised
